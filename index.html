Kasparov's draw with deep blue!

Deep Blue versus Garry Kasparov was a pair of six-game chess matches between the world chess
champion Garry Kasparov and an IBM supercomputer called Deep Blue. The first match was
played in Philadelphia in 1996 and won by Kasparov by 4–2. A rematch was played in
New York City in 1997 and won by Deep Blue by 3½–2½. The second match was the first
defeat of a reigning world chess champion by a computer under tournament conditions,
and was the subject of a documentary film, Game Over: Kasparov and the Machine.
